  1. Why I Love Vim

    Firstly, let’s talk about another question: what do you think about developers? Well, developers are lazy and clever at the same time. They often spend a large quantity of time thinking how to automate repetitive tasks so they can get more time to make innovations, such as pondering over the software design or doing refactoring. They don’t want to repeat themselves over and over again; they always try to make things with as less effort as possible to get as much things as possible done. They don’t want to waste time; they want to be productive. Vim is the best way for you to accomplish all these targets.

    Basically, vim is a text editor. But vim is much more than that. With vim, you can do almost everything for developing. No matter you are a frontend or a backend developer, you can always use vim to do your job. With vim, debuggers and version control system clients etc all suddenly become unnecessary; you can integrate them all with vim at once.

    Vim can help you work more smoothly and effectively. You can move your cursors between lines with just a few keystrokes, customize the entire editor so it fits your preferences best and do complex operations in the most effecient way with the help of all kinds of vim plugins. Embracing vim, you’ll be given the gift of making more progress in shorter times.

    I think you know that there’re so many people who have to work remotely through slow network connections to do their jobs on different computers. For them, vim is godsend. At first, vim is light weight and adequate of working in bad connections. And secondly vim is ubiquitous, you can find vim in almost every computer running Linux. Last but most significantly, vim can provide you with a unified experience in editing. You can easily configure vim in the remote computer to make it behave exactly as the one on your computer by just uploading one small file.

    Now you’ve appreciated the magic of vim, but as you know there is no such thing as a free lunch. Before puttering cozily around in the vim world, you have to totally exert yourself to get command of vim for the learning curve of vim is formidable. Believe me, it’s worth.
